# Chromebook Channels

Parallel to [apple-education-contacts.md](apple-education-contacts.md), but for the Chromebook side. Many U.S. middle schools — likely the majority at the middle-school level — are Chromebook-first. Mission HydroSci runs on both iPads and Chromebooks, so excluding Chromebook channels would cut a major fraction of eligible schools out of the campaign.

This was the largest channel gap in the original repo (the source material was iPad-heavy). Closing it is a Wave 2 priority.

> **Status note:** This file is a scaffold. Specific URLs, named contacts, and program details need to be researched. See [info-gathering-plan.md](info-gathering-plan.md) Priority 5.

---

## Why This Matters

- Among U.S. K–12 districts, Chromebook deployment scale exceeds iPad deployment scale, especially in upper-elementary and middle school grade bands.
- Many districts are **Chromebook-only** at the middle-school level — these schools cannot run iPad-only software.
- MHS is platform-flexible. Recruiting messaging that says "iPads or Chromebooks" is significantly more inclusive than "iPads"; this should be explicit in every outreach asset.
- Chromebook-side teacher communities (Google Certified Educators, Google for Education Innovators) are well-organized and a strong analog to the Apple Distinguished Educator network.

### What Google Will Do
Like Apple, Google for Education can connect researchers and partners with:
- Regional Google for Education representatives
- Solutions consultants
- Education-vertical sales engineers
- District relationship managers

For Mission HydroSci, useful conversations include:
- Which districts are running Chromebook 1:1 in middle schools at scale
- How Google's classroom integration tools (Google Classroom, Google Workspace for Education) could integrate with MHS
- Whether Google's K–12 newsletter has placement options

### What to Ask For
> "Education Solutions Consultant or Education Account Executive for K–12 districts running 1:1 Chromebook deployments in middle schools"

This is the Chromebook analog of the request to Apple Education. Aim for someone who works with districts directly rather than enterprise sales.

## Google Certified Educator Program

The clearest analog to Apple Distinguished Educators on the Chromebook side.

- **Program:** **VERIFY** current program structure (Google's certification programs have evolved over time — currently Google Certified Educator Level 1, Level 2, and Google for Education Certified Trainer / Coach / Innovator)
- **Audience:** teachers who have proactively pursued Google certification for K–12 use; very high engagement; research-friendly profile
- **How to engage:**
  1. Identify program contacts at Google for Education
  2. Ask about featuring MHS in Certified Educator newsletters or community channels
  3. Identify named Certified Educators teaching middle-school earth science (LinkedIn search)

---

## Google for Education Innovator Program

- **Program:** Google for Education Innovator (formerly Google Certified Innovator)
- **Audience:** highly selective — teachers admitted have demonstrated leadership in technology integration. Roughly the analog of an Apple Distinguished Educator.
- **Recruiting fit:** strong — these are exactly the teachers who would adopt and champion a research-backed game-based learning experience. Modest in number but high-leverage.
- **How to engage:** identify earth-science Innovators via the program directory; direct outreach with personalized notes.

## Common Sense Education

Closely tied to the Chromebook side because of strong web-based tool reviews and Google integrations.

- **Web:** `commonsense.org/education` **VERIFY**
- **What they do:** review and curate educational tools, including Chromebook-friendly web apps; large teacher audience
- **Channels to consider:**
  - Tool review submission (long lead time but high credibility)
  - Newsletter mention
  - Case study placement
- **Recruiting fit:** moderate; strongest as a credibility / discovery channel rather than a recruiting channel.

## Edtech Tool Marketplaces (Chromebook-Friendly)

Chromebook-side platforms that aggregate web-based educational tools — useful for discovery surface area:

- **Clever Library** — Chromebook-friendly app catalog; SSO integration
- **Classlink** — district SSO platform with app catalog

These are partnership / discovery channels rather than direct recruiting channels, but inclusion in any of them increases organic discoverability of MHS by Chromebook-running schools.
