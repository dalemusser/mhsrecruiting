# Pilot Teacher Engagement

The single highest-ROI move in the recruiting plan. Existing Mission HydroSci pilot teachers in Missouri are the cheapest, fastest source of credibility, testimonials, and peer referrals. This file is the playbook for activating them.

---

## Why This Matters

- **Lowest CAC.** Every other channel costs us time or money. A peer-to-peer teacher referral is essentially free and converts at multiples of cold outreach.
- **Highest credibility.** A teacher hearing from another teacher about a research project they actually ran is fundamentally different from a cold email from a research lead.
- **Critical assets.** Pilot teachers can produce testimonials, classroom photos, and short video clips that we need for the landing page, one-pager, and partner co-marketing — assets we can't get any other way.
- **Network multiplication.** If 10 pilot teachers each refer 3–5 peers, that's 30–50 warm leads — a meaningful fraction of our Fall 2026 cohort target — from one move.

---

## Action Items (Critical Path)

These need to happen in week 1 of the campaign.

1. **Get the pilot teacher list from the MHS team.** Names, schools, districts, contact info, role on the project (lead teacher / co-teacher / classroom test / focus group only). Owner: MHS team. Target date: 2026-05-02.
2. **Identify which pilots have given consent** to be named publicly, photographed, or video-recorded. IRB consent and FERPA implications.
3. **Draft a personalized outreach message per pilot** — this is not cold outreach, and it shouldn't read like it. Reference the specific work each pilot did.
4. **Schedule 20-minute calls with each pilot** during May 2026 — cheaper than asking for written input.

## Engagement Playbook

### Step 1: Initial Reconnect (May 2026)

Goal: re-establish contact and ask if they'd be willing to help recruit peers.

- **Channel:** email (followed by phone if no response in 5 business days)
- **Tone:** colleague catching up, not project staff making an ask
- **Specific asks (in order, low-friction first):**
  1. Would they be willing to refer 3–5 peer middle-school earth-science teachers?
  2. Would they let us quote them in recruiting materials (with their name + school)?
  3. Could we feature a classroom photo or 60-second video clip on the landing page?
  4. Would they be open to participating in a recruiting webinar (15-minute "what it was actually like" segment)?

### Step 2: Capture Artifacts (May–June 2026)

For each willing pilot, capture:
- **Testimonial quote** (2–3 sentences, attributable)
- **One concrete anecdote** (a specific student moment, a specific lesson outcome) — anecdotes outperform abstractions in recruiting messaging
- **Classroom photo** (consent permitting; FERPA-safe — ideally without identifiable students or with parental consent secured)
- **60–90 second video clip** (optional; for landing page and social)
- **Their device platform** — iPads / Chromebooks / both — so we can match the testimonial to the right prospects

### Step 3: Peer Referrals (May–July 2026)

For each willing pilot:
- Provide a short, forwardable email they can send to colleagues. (Template below.)
- Ask for 3–5 peer names + emails so we can follow up directly with attribution to the referrer.
- Track referral attribution in the tracking system (see [tracking-template.md](tracking-template.md)).

### Step 4: Webinar Participation (June–August 2026)

For 2–3 pilots willing to do a 15-minute live segment in our recruitment webinars:
- Their voice is more persuasive than ours for a teacher audience.
- Schedule them as the second half of the webinar — agenda, MHS overview, then teacher voice + Q&A.

## Outreach Template — Pilot Teacher Reconnect

> **Subject:** Mission HydroSci — quick favor (and an update)
>
> Hi [Name] —
>
> I hope your school year is winding down well. I wanted to reach back out because **Mission HydroSci** is moving into broader classroom deployment for **Fall 2026 and Spring 2027**, and we're recruiting middle-school earth-science teachers nationwide for the next two cohorts.
>
> Your work with us during the pilot has shaped the project in ways that will directly help these new teachers, and I wanted to ask whether you'd be willing to help with a few small things:
>
> 1. Would you be open to **referring 2–3 middle-school earth-science teachers** in your network who might be a good fit for the next cohort? I'd reach out to them directly — your only ask would be the introduction.
> 2. Would you let me **quote you (with your name and school)** in our recruiting materials? Even a short paragraph about your experience would be enormously helpful.
> 3. (Lower priority, only if interested) Would you be open to a brief appearance in a **recruiting webinar** for prospective teachers? 15 minutes of your time, mostly Q&A.
>
> If a 20-minute call works better than email for any of this, I'm happy to schedule. Whatever's easiest.
>
> Thanks again for everything you've done for the project.
>
> [Signature]

### What to Customize

- **Specific reference to their pilot work** — replace generic praise with one concrete thing they did. Pilot teachers know whether you remember their classroom or not.
- **The asks** — tailor based on what we know about their consent status. If they've already declined to be named, don't ask again; ask for referrals only.

## Forwardable Peer-Referral Email (For Pilots to Send)

A short, ready-to-forward email pilot teachers can send their colleagues. Goal: make the referral as low-friction as possible.

> **Subject:** Research project worth a look — Mission HydroSci
>
> Hi [Colleague] —
>
> I wanted to share something I've been part of that I think you'd like. **Mission HydroSci** is a Department of Education–funded research project from the University of Missouri. It's a game-based learning experience for middle school Earth Science that runs on iPads or Chromebooks. I taught with it last year and [one short personal sentence — "my students were genuinely engaged" / "it filled a real gap in my unit on watersheds" / etc.].
>
> They're recruiting middle-school earth-science teachers for **Fall 2026 and Spring 2027**. Participating teachers get a stipend, free curriculum and training, and ongoing support. I thought of you because [reason].
>
> If you're curious, here's the project home: https://mhs.missouri.edu — or I can connect you with the project lead directly. Just let me know.
>
> [Pilot teacher's name]

### How to Use This Template

- The MHS team supplies the template. The pilot teacher personalizes the bracketed fields and sends it to their own colleagues from their own email.
- Coming from the pilot's email rather than ours is the whole point — preserve that.

## Referral Incentive (For Consideration)

A modest referral incentive can meaningfully lift pilot engagement and quality of referrals. Recommended structure (for MHS team to evaluate):

- **$50 honorarium per referral that completes an application** — caps the program cost at a predictable amount and rewards quality, not volume.
- **Public recognition** — name pilot teachers as "Mission HydroSci Network" members on the project site. Many teachers value this more than the cash.
- **Free conference registration** for pilots who refer 5+ teachers — turns referrers into ambassadors.

If a referral incentive program is enabled, document the structure here and reflect it in the outreach template (line 1 of the asks).

## Risk and Considerations

- **IRB and FERPA.** Anything we publish — quotes, photos, video, classroom anecdotes — needs explicit consent from the pilot teacher and (for classroom photos involving students) appropriate FERPA-compliant consent. Confirm with the project's IRB before publishing.
- **Pilot teacher capacity.** Teachers are time-constrained, especially in May (end of school year). The lighter the ask, the higher the response rate. Lead with the smallest possible ask (referrals) and let them opt into more if interested.
- **Don't over-tap one teacher.** Spread asks across the pilot pool; if a pilot becomes a referrer + testimonial + webinar guest + advocate, recognize that we owe them substantial gratitude.
