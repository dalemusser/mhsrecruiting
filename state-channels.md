# State Channels

State-level surfaces for reaching earth-science middle-school teachers. Every state has the same basic structure — state science teacher association (NSTA chapter), state Department of Education science specialist, and (sometimes) regional educational service districts. Hitting the priority states first gives the best leverage.

> **Status note:** This is a scaffold. Specific named contacts and current URLs need to be researched per state. Round 1 covers the priority 10 states; round 2 covers the remaining 40. See [info-gathering-plan.md](info-gathering-plan.md) for effort estimates.

---

## How to Use Each State Entry

For each state, target three layers:

1. **State science teacher association** (NSTA chapter) — newsletter, annual conference, listservs. Most associations welcome research-recruitment content if presented well.
2. **State Department of Education science / NGSS specialist** — single named individual at the state DoE who oversees science curriculum, NGSS implementation, or science assessment. They know who's teaching earth science and often broker research participation.
3. **Regional educational service districts** — sub-state PD organizations (BOCES in NY, ESDs in WA, ROE in IL, etc.). Useful for batched teacher reach within a region.

## Outreach Pattern (Per State)

For each state, the outreach sequence is:

1. **Email the state science teacher association** — short message asking about newsletter inclusion or member-channel posting. Use Template 4 from [outreach-templates.md](outreach-templates.md) adapted to the network.
2. **Email the state DoE science specialist** — slightly more formal. Identify MHS as DoE-funded, ask about teacher recruitment channels in the state.
3. **Engage regional service districts** — only after state-level engagement; ask the association or DoE specialist for the highest-engagement regional contacts rather than cold-emailing all of them.

Cycle time per state for round 1 (priority states): ~2 hours of research + 1 hour of outreach.

## NGSS-Adoption Status (Useful for Framing)

States that have adopted or substantially adopted NGSS will respond more strongly to MHS's NGSS-aligned messaging. States with their own standards (TX, VA, FL, others) need slightly different framing:

- **NGSS-adopted (~20 states):** lead with NGSS alignment in outreach.
- **NGSS-influenced but with own standards:** lead with conceptual alignment, mention NGSS as cross-walk.
- **Distinct frameworks (TX TEKS, FL Sunshine, VA SOL):** lead with classroom outcomes; mention crosswalk to state standards.

NGSS adoption status by state should be confirmed during round 2 research (NextGenScience's website tracks this — see [university-and-partnership-channels.md](university-and-partnership-channels.md)).
