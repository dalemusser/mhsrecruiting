## Why This School Matters for MHS

- **Middle school grades 6–8 present.** Direct fit for MHS earth-science target audience.
- **SFUSD 1:1 Chromebook deployment.** Every middle-school student has a take-home Chromebook (see [sfusd.md](sfusd.md)). MHS runs on Chromebooks, so technology is not a barrier.
- **Filipino Education Center identity.** The school has a distinct cultural-education focus. MHS recruiting messaging should respect this — frame participation around how MHS serves their students' learning, not as generic district-wide outreach.
- **California priority.** California is one of our priority 10 states per [state-channels.md](state-channels.md). SF Bay Area schools also offer geographic proximity for in-person engagement (the project lead works from San Francisco — see project context).

## Outreach Approach

### Recommended Sequence

1. **Start with Dr. Santiago (Assistant Principal).** She's named as the tour contact, which suggests she's the public-facing administrator most comfortable engaging with outside inquiries. Email santiagoj@sfusd.edu.
2. **In parallel or as follow-up: Principal Anna Wayne.** waynea@sfusd.edu. Useful for institutional approval if Dr. Santiago wants leadership sign-off before connecting us with teachers.
3. **Once warm, ask for an introduction to the middle-school science / earth-science teacher(s).** Or visit the middle school main office (415-291-7983) directly.
4. **If geographically feasible, request a tour.** In-person visits convert at higher rates than email-only outreach for this kind of institutional engagement.

### Outreach Message Adaptation

Use the district-leader template from [outreach-templates.md](outreach-templates.md), with these specific tweaks:

- Reference SFUSD's 1:1 Chromebook deployment to immediately defuse any device-compatibility question.
- Note that MHS is San Francisco–local on the project lead's side, and an in-person visit / tour is offered.
- Frame the ask narrowly: "I'd like to learn whether the middle school's earth-science teacher(s) might be a fit for the Fall 2026 or Spring 2027 cohort."
- Do not pitch broadly to the school or district; focus the ask on individual teachers.

### Information to Gather on First Contact

- How many middle-school earth-science teachers are at the school
- Who they are (names, ideally email contact)
- The middle-school science scheduling pattern (which grades cover earth-science, when in the year)
- Any SFUSD-level approval gates for outside research participation (often districts require central approval for research even when a school is willing)
