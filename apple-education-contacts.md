# Apple Education Contacts

Apple's education channel is one of the most efficient ways to access cross-district patterns and warm introductions. Apple sees patterns across many districts that no individual district can — keyboard adoption rates, trackpad usage, device generation mix, deployment realities. For an iPad-supporting research project like Mission HydroSci, Apple's education contacts are higher-leverage than reaching out to most individual districts cold.

This file is iPad-specific by nature; for Chromebook-heavy districts, separate channels are needed (Google for Education would be the analog).

---

## Direct Entry Points

### General Education Sales
- **Phone:** 1-800-800-2775

### Email
- **State / local education sales:** statelocalsales@apple.com
- **Contracts:** contracts@apple.com

These are official Apple channels — not marketing-only fluff.

---

## What Apple Will Do (and Why It's Valuable)

If contacted correctly, Apple will:

- Connect you with **regional education reps**, **solution engineers**, and **deployment specialists**
- Provide insight into:
  - Real usage patterns
  - Device fleet data
  - Accessory usage trends
  - App deployment realities

For Mission HydroSci, the most useful conversations are with people who can answer "how are iPads actually being used in middle school earth-science classrooms across multiple districts?" — Apple's regional and education-vertical staff are well-positioned for that.

---

## What to Ask For

When calling or emailing, ask specifically for:

> "K–12 Education Solutions Engineer or Education Account Executive for [region] districts"

That request gets past generic sales and reaches operational education staff.

For MHS specifically, useful targets are:

- **Education Solutions Engineer** — technical depth on deployment, MDM, device fleet realities
- **Education Account Executive** — knows district-level relationships and can make warm introductions
- **Apple Distinguished Educator program contacts** — gateway to teachers who are actively using iPads in research-friendly ways

---

## Apple Distinguished Schools / Educators

Apple Distinguished Schools (visible in the Apple Education success-stories pages) tend to:

- Have strong institutional support for digital learning research
- Publish staff contacts more openly
- Already be running 1:1 iPad programs at scale

The Apple Education success-stories landing page (https://www.apple.com/education/k12/success-stories/) is a useful starting point for identifying U.S. Distinguished Schools that might host MHS pilots.

---

## Apple Education / Industry Experts (Indirect Path)

A useful complement to direct Apple outreach is connecting with **former Apple Education leadership and consultants** — people who worked directly with districts. They are often easier to reach than Apple staff and can speak candidly about:

- Keyboard usage rates
- MDM realities
- Classroom constraints
- What deployment patterns actually look like at scale

LinkedIn searches for "former Apple education," "Apple education strategist," or "K–12 education consultant Apple" surface this profile.

---

## Recommended First Move

If a single Apple-side outreach action were prioritized for MHS:

1. **Email statelocalsales@apple.com** with a short, specific message:
   - Identify MHS as a Department of Education–funded research project from the University of Missouri.
   - Specify the target audience (middle school earth-science teachers, U.S.).
   - Specify the deployment timing (Fall 2026 / Spring 2027).
   - Ask for an introduction to a K–12 Education Solutions Engineer or Education Account Executive who works with districts running 1:1 iPad programs in middle schools.

2. **In parallel, post on LinkedIn** to surface former Apple Education contacts in your network or one degree out — these conversations are usually faster than the official channel.
