# District Contacts & Examples

Named district-level contacts and example districts running compatible technology (iPads, Chromebooks, or both). MHS runs on either platform, so any district where students have adequate technology is in scope for recruiting.

When recording new entries, capture the **device platform** (iPads / Chromebooks / both) and the **deployment scale** so we can prioritize districts where MHS would actually be deployable in classrooms.

---

## Tier-Ranked Contact Strategy

Across any district, the most useful titles to target are:

- **Director of Educational Technology**
- **Instructional Technology Coordinator**
- **Digital Learning Specialist**
- **Technology Integration Coach**
- **IT Director (K–12)**

These are the operational decision-makers who know real classroom usage. CIOs and Superintendents are too high-level for MHS recruiting conversations; teacher-facing IT and instructional-technology leadership is the right altitude.

**Three perspectives are valuable for cross-checking:**

1. **District EdTech leadership** — strategy and district-wide patterns
2. **MDM / device-management admins** — implementation realities and constraints
3. **Teachers using devices daily** — what actually works in the classroom

If we can talk to two of the three for any target district, our assumptions for that district become high-confidence.

## Smaller / Regional U.S. District Examples

Smaller districts are usually more approachable than LAUSD-scale systems and often publish staff contacts directly.

### Girard USD (Kansas)
- **Site:** https://www.girard248.org/
- **Device platform:** iPads (iPad 9th Generation, with keyboard cases — no trackpads noted)
- **Why it matters:** Confirms real-world device usage and accessory variability; a good example of a smaller district with a structured 1:1 program.

### Hamilton Southeastern Schools (Indiana)
- **Site:** https://fcj.hseschools.org/academics/tech-in-the-classroom
- **Device platform:** iPads
- **Focus:** Digital curriculum, interactive learning
- **Why it matters:** Representative of a modern suburban district deployment.

### Kirby School District (Illinois)
- **Site:** https://www.ksd140.org/news/
- **Device platform:** iPads (iPad 10th gen; device-only option — confirms some students have no keyboard at all)
- **Why it matters:** Confirms minimum-viable deployment shape for an MHS classroom (iPad-only, touch-only).

## Critical Insight

The real knowledge is not centralized:

- **Apple** knows patterns across districts.
- **District IT** knows constraints.
- **Teachers** know actual usage.

For MHS recruiting, the most efficient path is usually to reach teachers directly (via the channels in [teacher-resource-sites.md](teacher-resource-sites.md) and [professional-networks.md](professional-networks.md)) and only engage district leadership when teacher participation requires district-level approval.
