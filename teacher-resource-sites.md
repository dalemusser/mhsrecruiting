# Teacher Resource Sites

Teacher-authored websites and earth-science resource hubs that reach middle school science teachers. These are high-leverage targets for MHS recruiting because they reflect real classroom practice, connect directly to teacher audiences, and surface NGSS-aligned, earth-science–relevant content.

---

## Tier 1 — Teacher-Authored Sites

These are the highest-value outreach targets because they are run by working middle school science teachers and have direct teacher audiences.

### Sunrise Science Classroom (Karla)
- **Site:** https://sunrisescienceclassroom.com/
- **Created by:** A middle school science teacher with 15+ years of experience
- **Focus:** NGSS-aligned lessons, Earth & Space Science, classroom-ready materials
- **Reach:** Used by 90,000+ teachers
- **Why it matters for MHS:** Direct access to a large teacher audience aligned with our content area; the site reflects what teachers actually use day-to-day in earth-science classrooms.

### Science Spot
- **Site:** https://sciencespot.net
- **Created by:** A middle school teacher
- **Includes:** Lesson plans, labs, NGSS-aligned resources
- **Why it matters for MHS:** Long-running teacher-to-teacher hub; reflects grassroots middle school science needs and is a natural place to surface MHS to teachers actively looking for inquiry-based earth-science material.

## Tier 2 — Earth-Science–Focused Resource Hubs

### Middle School Earth Science Resource Directory (CRScience)
- **Site:** https://crscience.org/msearthwebsites/
- **Contact:** https://crscience.org/about/contactus/
- **Details:** Curated list of earth-science teaching tools organized by student activities, teacher background, and NGSS alignment
- **Why it matters for MHS:** Directly aligned with our domain; shows what teachers rely on (USGS data, NASA tools, climate resources). Inclusion here would put MHS in front of teachers actively browsing for earth-science material.

### CK-12 Foundation
- **Site:** https://www.ck12.org
- **Reach:** 200M+ students globally
- **Provides:** Earth Science textbooks, simulations, practice systems
- **Why it matters for MHS:** Common in middle school classrooms; represents the baseline expectation for digital earth-science content. A useful reference point for how teachers expect digital science material to work.

### Science Buddies
- **Site:** https://www.sciencebuddies.org
- **Org:** Nonprofit supporting K–12 science
- **Reach:** Used by millions of students and teachers
- **Why it matters for MHS:** A common entry point for project-based and inquiry-based science work — a natural audience for MHS-style learning.

## What These Sites Tell Us About Teachers

Patterns observed across the sites above:

- Teachers rely heavily on curated lesson sites, pre-built activities, and NGSS alignment tools.
- Earth-science teaching uses real-world data (USGS, NASA), simulations and visualization tools, and inquiry-based activities.
- Technology usage is inconsistent across schools — mixed device ecosystems, heavy reliance on browser-based tools.
- Teachers are time-constrained and prefer ready-to-use materials with clear standards alignment; they value student engagement and conceptual understanding over rote answers.

These patterns inform how MHS should be presented to teachers: lead with NGSS alignment, classroom-readiness, time-to-first-use, and conceptual / scientific reasoning outcomes — not with technology novelty.

---

## Outreach Notes

- Teacher-authored sites typically have a contact form or visible email. Cold outreach is generally welcomed when the message is short, specific to their audience, and offers something concrete (research participation, free access, or co-creation of materials).
- For resource directories like CRScience, the path to inclusion is usually a short submission email describing MHS, its NGSS alignment, and the participation model.
- For high-traffic platforms like CK-12 and Science Buddies, partnership outreach is a longer conversation — useful as a longer-horizon channel rather than a primary recruiting funnel for Fall 2026 / Spring 2027.
