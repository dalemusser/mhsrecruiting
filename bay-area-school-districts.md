# Bay Area School Districts

A directory of K–12 public school districts in the San Francisco Bay Area, organized by county. Targeted at the **East Bay** (Alameda + Contra Costa counties) and the **SF–San Jose corridor** (San Francisco + San Mateo + Santa Clara counties).

This is a starting reference for MHS recruiting. The Bay Area has ~80 public school districts across these five counties; this file lists them with grade ranges, websites, and MHS-recruiting fit. Specific district profiles (with named contacts and outreach status) live in standalone files — see the District and School Profiles section in [README.md](README.md).

---

## Reading Guide

### Why this directory matters for MHS

- **Geographic proximity.** The MHS project lead is based in San Francisco. Bay Area districts are uniquely accessible for in-person engagement (tours, principal meetings, classroom visits) — leverage where possible.
- **California priority state.** California is one of the priority 10 states per [state-channels.md](state-channels.md). NGSS-adopted; CSTA reach.
- **Scale.** The five-county region serves hundreds of middle schools — a meaningful recruiting pool.
- **Device platform variety.** Both iPad and Chromebook deployments exist; some districts are 1:1 (e.g., SFUSD), others are need-based (e.g., OUSD). Confirm device platform per district before recruiting at scale.

### MHS-fit criteria

For each district, the recruiting-fit shorthand:

- **★ In scope** — district includes middle-school grades (6–8); MHS classroom recruiting feasible
- **★★ Priority** — large district with many middle schools, or geographic / strategic advantage
- **— Out of scope** — high-school-only districts (no middle-school grades; not MHS-recruitable)
- **K–8** — elementary district whose upper grades (6–8) are MHS-eligible

### URL verification status

Some URLs in this file are confirmed; others are marked **VERIFY** when not directly verified during initial research. Confirm before outreach — California school district URLs change occasionally as districts migrate from `.k12.ca.us` to `.org` / `.net` domains.

## Approach for Bay Area Recruiting

The Bay Area concentration enables a different recruiting approach than national outreach:

1. **In-person engagement.** Tours, principal visits, classroom observations are feasible. Lead with this advantage — it converts meaningfully better than email-only.
2. **Sequential profiling.** Add standalone profile files for Tier 2 priority districts as research progresses, matching the [sfusd.md](sfusd.md) / [ousd.md](ousd.md) pattern. Each profile should capture device platform, key administrators, middle-school list, and outreach status.
3. **Snowball within district.** Once a teacher in one Bay Area district participates, ask for referrals to other Bay Area districts — same regional context, similar school constraints, easier to refer.
4. **Conferences and events.** Bay Area teachers attend CSTA, BACR (Bay Area conferences), and ISTE. Cross-reference with [conferences-and-events.md](conferences-and-events.md).
5. **University of Missouri alumni in the Bay Area.** Many Mizzou alumni teach in California; the Bay Area concentration enables targeted alumni outreach (see [university-and-partnership-channels.md](university-and-partnership-channels.md)).
