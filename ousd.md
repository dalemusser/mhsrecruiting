# Oakland Unified School District (OUSD)

A large urban public school district in Alameda County, California, serving the City of Oakland. OUSD has 15 middle schools serving grades 6–8 (plus a few K–8 and 6–12 schools that include middle grades) — a meaningful pool of potential MHS recruiting targets.

OUSD's Chromebook program is **different from SFUSD's**: rather than a 1:1 district-wide deployment, OUSD provides **need-based home-loan Chromebooks** to grades 6–12 students who indicate during registration that they lack home computer access. This nuance matters for MHS deployment planning — see "Device Platform" below.

## Device Platform: Home-Loan Chromebooks (Grades 6–12)

OUSD's publicly documented Chromebook program is **need-based and home-loan oriented**, not a 1:1 district-wide deployment.

### Two Programs

1. **District-Loaned Chromebooks (Current)** — devices for home use that must be returned. Available to students in grades 6–12 who meet **both** criteria:
   - Parent indicated during Data Confirmation (registration) that the student lacks home computer access
   - Student doesn't already have a device checked out
2. **#OaklandUndivided Chromebooks (Legacy)** — permanent devices distributed 2020–2022. Currently distributed only to Adult Education students.

### Loan Terms

- District-loaned devices require completion of an "OUSD Offsite Loan Agreement" prior to checkout.
- Devices are expected to be returned at the end of the loan period.
- No specific summer-retention policy is documented (contrast with SFUSD, which retains devices through the summer for transitioning students).

### Support

- **First contact:** the student's school handles login issues, hardware damage, and lost/stolen devices.
- **OUSD Tech Repair Hubs** provide secondary support.
- **Tech Exchange Tech Hub** offers free digital skills training and computers to families without home devices.
- **Device Support Wizard** at https://www.ousd.org/familycentral/home-technology-access/device-support-wizard — interactive triage for the three OUSD device types (district Chromebook, #OaklandUndivided Chromebook, district T-Mobile hotspot).

### Implication for MHS — Important

OUSD's home-loan model means the documented Chromebook program does **not** guarantee that every middle-school student has a device. **Whether MHS can be deployed in an OUSD classroom depends on the in-classroom device availability at the specific school**, which is a separate question from home access.

For each prospective OUSD school, **confirm before recruiting the teacher**:

- Are there enough Chromebooks (or other devices) available during science class for every student?
- If devices are shared (cart-based or stored in-classroom), is the schedule compatible with multi-session MHS gameplay?
- Does the school have an alternative device platform we should know about (some OUSD schools may have iPads or mixed deployments)?

This is the key recruiting-fit question for OUSD that doesn't apply for SFUSD.

## Why OUSD Is a Priority Target

1. **Scale.** ~15 middle schools in the district, each with its own science department — meaningful recruiting surface area.
2. **California priority state.** Per [state-channels.md](state-channels.md). Same statewide context as SFUSD (CSTA, NGSS-adopted, CDE).
3. **Geographic proximity to project lead.** The MHS project lead is based in San Francisco; Oakland is across the bay, accessible for in-person engagement (tours, principal meetings, classroom visits). This is a meaningful advantage relative to non-Bay-Area districts.
4. **Diverse student body.** OUSD serves a highly diverse student population — important for the research's external validity.
5. **Distinct from SFUSD.** Adding OUSD to the campaign doubles the Bay Area middle-school footprint without doubling the geographic outreach cost.

## Outreach Approach

### Two-Track Strategy (Same Pattern as SFUSD)

1. **School-by-school direct outreach.** Profile and reach individual OUSD middle schools (priority TBD by neighborhood and proximity), then approach school administrators and middle-school science teachers.
2. **District-level approval and visibility.** Engage OUSD's research office (TO RESEARCH) for formal approval. Explore whether OUSD instructional-technology leadership is interested in district-wide visibility for MHS.

### Recommended Starting Points

**For initial outreach, prioritize schools that are easiest to engage:**

- **Edna Brewer Middle School** — well-regarded, central Oakland location
- **Montera Middle School** — Oakland Hills, established science program likely
- **Bret Harte Middle School** — established, multiple-grade site
- **West Oakland Middle School** — relatively new (rebuilt program), may be more receptive to research collaboration

These are starting hypotheses; actual prioritization should be informed by:

- Which schools have publicly identifiable middle-school science / earth-science teachers
- Which schools have published technology / device-deployment information
- Any warm intros from existing MHS connections in the Bay Area

### Outreach Sequence Per School

For each target OUSD school:

1. **Identify the principal and assistant principal** via the school's OUSD-subdomain website
2. **Identify the middle-school science department lead or earth-science teacher(s)** — check school site staff directory
3. **Confirm in-classroom Chromebook (or device) availability** — important per the OUSD device-program nuance above
4. **Initial email outreach** to the most accessible administrator (use Template 1 from [outreach-templates.md](outreach-templates.md) adapted for OUSD)
5. **Request introduction to the earth-science teacher** if administrator engages

### Key Differences from SFUSD Approach

- **Confirm device availability per school** (not assumed district-wide as with SFUSD's 1:1 model)
- **OUSD research approval process is separate** from SFUSD's; do not assume parity
- **Cross-bay travel for in-person engagement** is feasible — leverage where possible
