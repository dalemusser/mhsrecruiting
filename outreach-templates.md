# Outreach Templates

Reusable templates for cold outreach to district leadership, Apple Education contacts, teachers, and PD network leaders. Each template should be lightly tailored per recipient — the structure is reusable; the specifics matter.

---

## Template 1: District EdTech Leader (Cold)

This template originated from a message drafted to **Dominic Caguioa** at LAUSD (Director of Educational Technology & Innovation). It works well for any district instructional-technology lead.

> **Subject:** Department of Education Project — Mission HydroSci (Quick Discussion)
>
> Hi [Name] —
>
> I'm currently working on a Department of Education–funded project called **Mission HydroSci**, an interactive, game-based learning experience designed to help students build scientific reasoning and problem-solving skills through inquiry-driven gameplay on Chromebooks and iPads.
>
> The core research compares student learning in Earth Science topics between gameplay and traditional instruction, and we're also exploring how AI can be used to generate more meaningful feedback from student decisions — going beyond correctness to better understand how students are thinking.
>
> As we've been preparing for broader classroom use, I've been trying to ground the design in what actually exists in schools (devices, input methods, deployment realities, etc.). [District name] is one of the systems I keep coming back to in thinking about this.
>
> If you have a few minutes at some point, I'd be interested in your perspective — especially around how something like this fits with what you're seeing in classrooms, and whether there might be a path to connect with teachers interested in participating.
>
> The project is being led out of the Colleges of Education and Engineering at the **University of Missouri** (PI: Jim Laffey). I'm a former faculty member there and currently working with the team from San Francisco. We're piloting in Missouri now and planning broader classroom deployments for **Fall 2026** and **Spring 2027**.
>
> More info:
> https://dalemusser.com
> https://mhs.missouri.edu
> https://intelligencebuilders.com
>
> Thanks,
> Dale
>
> (415) 279-4040
> dale@intelligencebuilders.com
> musserda@missouri.edu

### What to Customize

- **Recipient name** — first name only is fine for this register
- **District/school reference** — the "[District name] is one of the systems I keep coming back to" line. Replace with a specific, true reason this district matters (size, distinguished-school status, public visibility, prior research, etc.). Generic flattery is obvious; specificity isn't.
- **Subject line** — short and informational outperforms clever
- **Geography** — drop "from San Francisco" for non-California recipients if irrelevant; keep if you'd be available to visit

### Why This Works

- Frames MHS as **research**, not as a sales pitch
- Names the funder (Department of Education) and institutional home (University of Missouri) early — this is the credibility signal that gets the message read
- Specifies the **device platform** (Chromebooks and iPads), which signals MHS is broadly deployable rather than iPad-exclusive
- Closes with a low-friction ask ("a few minutes at some point") rather than a meeting request
- Includes multiple email addresses and a phone number — recipient picks the channel

> - Project home: https://mhs.missouri.edu
> - Led from the University of Missouri Colleges of Education and Engineering (PI: Jim Laffey)
> - I'm working with the team from San Francisco
>
> Would you be open to a short call to learn more, or would you like me to send a one-page summary first?
>
> Thanks,
> Dale Musser
> dale@intelligencebuilders.com / musserda@missouri.edu
> (415) 279-4040

### What to Customize

- **Specific reason for reaching out** — this is the part that makes the message land. Always include a concrete, true reason you contacted *this person*.
- **Device-platform line** — keep "iPads and Chromebooks" inclusive; only narrow if you know the specific platform of their school.

## General Outreach Principles

- **Always say "research"** when it's research. It is the single biggest credibility signal versus a sales pitch.
- **Always lead with institutional affiliation** (DoE-funded, University of Missouri). This determines whether the message is read past the first sentence.
- **Always specify device platform inclusively** ("iPads and Chromebooks") unless you have a reason to narrow.
- **Always give the recipient a low-friction option** — one-pager, short call, async email reply. Never default to "let's get a meeting on the calendar."
- **Always include multiple contact channels** (email + phone). It costs nothing and lifts response rates.
- **Always tailor the specific reason for reaching out**. Generic outreach is recognizable in two seconds and gets archived.
