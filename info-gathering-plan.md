# Information-Gathering Plan

The recruiting strategy depends on a channel inventory we don't fully have yet. This file is the research roadmap — what to look up, in what order, and which file each piece of research feeds.

Each item below is structured as: **what to research**, **why it matters**, **deliverable file**, **priority**, and **rough effort**.

---

## Priority 1 — Earth-Science Organizations
**Deliverable:** [earth-science-organizations.md](earth-science-organizations.md)
**Effort:** ~1 day
**Why first:** highest single-channel ROI. NESTA members are *exactly* our target audience, and a single newsletter mention can reach thousands of relevant teachers.

For each of NESTA, NAGT, AGI, AGU Education, NSTA earth-science section, find:

- Public website URL
- Membership size (rough order of magnitude is fine)
- Newsletter or members-only communication channels
- Conference / event calendar for 2026
- Named education or member-services contact (LinkedIn or org website)
- Research-recruitment policy (do they accept research-recruitment posts? on what terms?)
- Recent K–12 or middle-school earth-science programs that would suggest fit

---

## Priority 2 — Conferences and Events Calendar
**Deliverable:** [conferences-and-events.md](conferences-and-events.md)
**Effort:** ~half day
**Why second:** several 2026 fall conferences likely have early-summer registration / proposal deadlines. Missing a deadline kills a channel for the cohort.

Look up dates, locations, deadlines for:

- NSTA national 2026 (likely past, but capture for 2027) and 2026 fall regionals (typically Boston/Atlanta/Long Beach rotation)
- NESTA workshops at NSTA conferences
- NESTA standalone events
- ISTE 2026
- Games + Learning + Society (GLS) 2026
- Games for Change (G4C) 2026
- Major state science teacher association fall conferences (start with CA, TX, NY, FL, IL — the largest)

For each: dates, location, registration deadline, exhibit/sponsorship options, presenter call-for-proposals deadlines, exhibitor cost, attendee count and profile.

## Priority 3 — State Channels (Top 10 States First)
**Deliverable:** [state-channels.md](state-channels.md)
**Effort:** ~1.5 days for 10 states; ~3 days for all 50
**Why third:** state associations are the largest concentrated teacher channel. Hitting 10 priority states first gives the best leverage in the campaign window.

Priority states for first-pass research (based on size, NGSS leadership, and MHS connections):

1. **Missouri** (existing MHS pilot connections)
2. **California** (largest; NGSS-leading)
3. **Texas** (large)
4. **New York** (large)
5. **Florida** (large)
6. **Illinois** (large)
7. **Pennsylvania** (large)
8. **Ohio** (large)
9. **Massachusetts** (NGSS-leading)
10. **Washington** (NGSS-leading)

For each state:

- State science teacher association name + URL + newsletter contact
- State Department of Education science / NGSS / earth-science specialist (named, with email)
- Major regional educational service districts (BOCES in NY, ESDs in WA, etc.)
- Apple Distinguished Schools and/or major Chromebook districts in that state (cross-reference with [district-contacts.md](district-contacts.md))

Round 2 covers the remaining 40 states; can be lower-touch (just the association + DoE specialist).

## Priority 4 — Online Teacher Communities
**Deliverable:** [online-teacher-communities.md](online-teacher-communities.md)
**Effort:** ~half day
**Why fourth:** scales easily but requires careful etiquette. Cheaper than conferences, broader than newsletters.

Research:

- **Facebook groups:** find earth-science middle-school teacher groups. Document each group's name, member count, posting rules (research recruitment allowed?), and admin contact for permission.
- **Reddit:** r/ScienceTeachers, r/Teachers, r/NGSS — review subreddit rules; some forbid research recruitment, others allow with mod approval.
- **Twitter / X:** schedule of #ngsschat (typically Thursday evenings); list of earth-science teacher influencers (1k–10k follower range) worth engaging.
- **Teachers Pay Teachers:** identify top 10–20 earth-science middle-school authors. Many are influential on social media beyond TpT.
- **Edutopia and We Are Teachers:** comment thread engagement and contributor reach.

## Priority 5 — Chromebook Channels
**Deliverable:** [chromebook-channels.md](chromebook-channels.md)
**Effort:** ~half day
**Why fifth:** large channel gap currently. Many U.S. middle schools are Chromebook-first; without this, we miss a meaningful fraction of eligible schools.

Research:

- **Google for Education** — sales / contracts / solutions engineer entry points (analog of `apple-education-contacts.md`)
- **Google Certified Educator program** — gateway to motivated, research-friendly teachers
- **Google for Education Innovator program** — ADE-equivalent for the Google side
- **Major Chromebook districts** at U.S. middle-school level — to populate `district-contacts.md` Chromebook section
- **Common Sense Education** — teacher-facing org with strong Chromebook tooling reach

## Priority 6 — University and Partnership Channels
**Deliverable:** [university-and-partnership-channels.md](university-and-partnership-channels.md)
**Effort:** ~1 day
**Why sixth:** warm-intro pathways. Slower to activate than direct teacher outreach, but produce higher-quality leads.

Research:

- **University of Missouri College of Education** — alumni network, current K–12 outreach office, science education faculty contacts
- **Other Mizzou DoE-funded education research projects** — peer projects with teacher networks
- **BSCS Science Learning** — NGSS curriculum org with deep teacher networks
- **NextGenScience / Achieve** — NGSS standards organizations with state-level contacts
- **WestEd Science Learning** — assessment + curriculum org with teacher reach
- **Apple Distinguished Educators / Schools** — gateway via [apple-education-contacts.md](apple-education-contacts.md)
- **NSF-funded earth-science education projects** — peer projects (find via NSF Award Search for relevant DRK-12 grants)
- **Major K12 science publishers** — Pearson, McGraw-Hill, Cengage, HMH — possible co-marketing, especially for publishers selling earth-science middle-school curriculum

## Research Methods

### Sources
- Org websites and About / Contact pages (always start here)
- LinkedIn for named contacts
- NSF Award Search (https://www.nsf.gov/awardsearch/) for peer projects
- Wikipedia for org overviews
- State DoE websites for science specialists
- Search "[state] science teacher association" for state chapters
- ERIC (https://eric.ed.gov) for related research projects
- Twitter / X for community surface area

### Verification
- Confirm any named individual is currently in role (LinkedIn last-updated)
- Confirm any URL is current (visit, don't trust cached data)
- Note last-verified date in the deliverable file so we know when it's stale

### What to Skip
- Don't go deep on K–12 channels unrelated to middle-school earth science (e.g., elementary literacy networks — out of scope)
- Don't spend time mapping every state's Chromebook deployment — sample top 10
- Don't try to map all 50 state DoE science specialists in pass 1; do the priority 10 first

---

## Output Discipline

For each deliverable file:

- Lead with a 1-sentence purpose statement
- Group entries by tier (best targets first)
- For each entry: name, URL, contact (if known), why it matters, how to engage, last-verified date
- End with "Gaps / Next Steps" — flag what's still unknown so the next researcher knows where to pick up

This is research output, not strategy — keep entries factual and actionable. Strategy decisions belong in [recruiting-strategy.md](recruiting-strategy.md).
