# Online Teacher Communities

Where earth-science middle-school teachers congregate online. These are scalable, low-cost channels — but each has its own etiquette, and most explicitly forbid spammy promotion. Done right, a single well-crafted post can reach thousands of relevant teachers and produce higher-quality leads than paid channels.

> **Status note:** This file is a scaffold. Specific group names, member counts, and posting rules need to be researched per platform. See [info-gathering-plan.md](info-gathering-plan.md) Priority 4.

---

## Working Principles

Across every platform below:

- **Lead with research, not recruitment.** The most effective framing is "We're a DoE-funded research project from the University of Missouri studying middle-school earth-science learning, and we're recruiting teachers for Fall 2026 / Spring 2027."
- **Respect community rules.** Most teacher communities have explicit rules about self-promotion. Read each group's pinned posts and rules before posting; ask moderators when in doubt.
- **Show, don't tell.** A short demo screenshot or video clip in the post outperforms a wall of text.
- **Make the next step low-friction.** End every post with a single clear link — to the landing page or the application form.

## Facebook Groups

Most U.S. middle-school teachers are active in 1–3 Facebook groups specific to their teaching. These are some of the densest concentrations of our target audience.

### Groups to research and engage
- **"Middle School Science Teachers"** (large, general; multiple groups with similar names — find the largest active one)
- **"NGSS Teachers"** (NGSS-focused; earth-science is one of three strands)
- **"Earth Science Teachers"** (smaller, directly aligned)
- **"Middle School Earth Science"** (direct match if one exists at scale)
- **"Teachers of Watershed / Hydrology"** or similar (niche, but high alignment with MHS-specific content)
- **State-specific science teacher groups** (one per priority state)

For each group, document:
- Group name and URL
- Approximate member count
- Posting rules (admin-approved? "no promotion" rule? research recruitment specifically allowed?)
- Admin contact for permission requests
- Posting cadence norms

### Engagement playbook
1. **First post:** introduction + research-recruitment ask. Get admin permission first if rules are unclear.
2. **Follow-up engagement:** answer questions, post updates, share testimonials from pilot teachers.
3. **No promotional reposts.** One initial post is fine; repeated posting will get the project banned and damage credibility.

## Reddit

Reddit is smaller for teachers than Facebook but engaged users skew tech-friendly and research-curious — well-aligned with MHS.

### Subreddits
- **r/ScienceTeachers** — direct match; check rules for research-recruitment permission
- **r/Teachers** — broader; moderate fit
- **r/NGSS** — small but exact fit if active
- **r/Education** — mostly policy discussion; lower priority

### Engagement playbook
1. **Read the rules of each subreddit.** Many forbid recruitment without mod approval.
2. **Message moderators first** for r/ScienceTeachers and r/NGSS — explain the project and ask permission to post.
3. **Post format:** title clearly states the recruitment ask and the funding source. Body has 3–4 short paragraphs with a clear CTA.
4. **Engage in comments.** Reddit rewards responsiveness; abandoning a post tanks future credibility.

## Twitter / X

The science-teacher community on Twitter is smaller than its peak but still active, especially around scheduled chats.

### Hashtags and chats to engage
- **#ngsschat** — usually weekly chats (typically Thursday evenings); active community of NGSS-implementing teachers
- **#scied** — broader science education community
- **#earthscience** — content-specific; some teacher use
- **#mschat** — middle-school teachers specifically
- **#sciencetwitter** — research-leaning

### Engagement playbook
1. **Follow before posting.** Spend a week observing #ngsschat before participating.
2. **Participate first, recruit second.** Show up to a chat or two with substantive contributions before posting a recruitment thread.
3. **Recruitment thread:** 3–4 tweets with screenshots and a clear CTA. Tag relevant accounts (e.g., NSTA, NESTA, NGSS).
4. **Pin the recruitment thread** to the project's Twitter account if one exists.

## Teachers Pay Teachers (TpT)

TpT authors are influential teachers — often the same people who run Sunrise Science, Science Spot, etc. The top earth-science authors have substantial followings inside and outside TpT.

### Targets to research
- **Top 10–20 earth-science middle-school authors on TpT** — search "earth science middle school" and sort by ratings/sales
- **Authors whose store names are also their public brand** (e.g., a teacher whose TpT store is "ScienceWithMrs[Name]" likely has Instagram, blog, or Pinterest presence)

### Engagement playbook
1. **Direct email** through their TpT store contact form. Brief, research-framed message.
2. **Offer something concrete:** ask about a paid newsletter mention, a guest blog post, or a co-marketing opportunity.
3. **Don't expect free promotion.** TpT authors are running businesses; budget for paid placements with a few high-reach authors.

## Edutopia and We Are Teachers

Major teacher-facing publications with significant earth-science teacher readership.

### Edutopia
- **Approach:** pitch a guest article or commentary about MHS for inclusion in their content calendar. Editorial approval needed.
- **Lead time:** typically months; useful for Spring 2027 recruiting.

### We Are Teachers
- **Approach:** content sponsorship and editorial features. Less academic than Edutopia; more accessible voice.
- **Lead time:** weeks to months; sponsored content available faster.

### Engagement playbook
- These are less "communities" than publishing channels. Best for Wave 5 (contingency) and Spring 2027.
- Budget required for paid content; editorial submissions are slower but free.

### Use cases

## Etiquette Don'ts

Things that tank credibility in teacher communities:

- Posting the same message in multiple groups simultaneously
- Posting without reading the group's pinned rules
- Failing to respond to comments on your own post
- Generic outreach that doesn't acknowledge the community's specific norms
- Promoting the project without disclosing funding source and institutional affiliation
- Returning to repost without adding new information
