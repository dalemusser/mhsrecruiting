# University and Partnership Channels

Warm-intro and partnership pathways for recruiting. These are slower to activate than direct teacher outreach but produce higher-quality leads and can yield ongoing distribution channels rather than one-shot reach.

> **Status note:** This file is a scaffold. Specific contacts and current program details need to be researched. See [info-gathering-plan.md](info-gathering-plan.md) Priority 6.

## Tier 1 — University of Missouri

The most underused asset in the recruiting plan. Mizzou alumni teach in every state; the College of Education has active K–12 networks; the project is institutionally a Mizzou project. Warm intros from Mizzou should be cheaper, faster, and higher-converting than any cold channel.

### MU College of Education
- **Engagement:**
  - Talk to the Dean's office about communicating MHS recruiting to alumni
  - Identify the College's K–12 outreach office and its existing teacher networks
  - Engage current science education faculty about peer connections at other institutions
- **Specific channels:**
  - College of Education alumni newsletter
  - College of Education social media
  - K–12 Office of Educational Outreach (or equivalent — verify current org structure)
- **Named contact:** **TO RESEARCH** — current Dean's office staff handling alumni and outreach

### MU College of Engineering
- **Why it matters:** MHS is led jointly out of Education and Engineering. Engineering has its own K–12 STEM outreach networks, often distinct from Education's.
- **Engagement:** identify engineering K–12 outreach staff; ask about leveraging their existing teacher PD networks

### MU Office of Research / VPR
- **Why it matters:** Mizzou's Office of the Vice President for Research often runs cross-college research participation outreach
- **Engagement:** ask about other DoE-funded education research peer projects at Mizzou; co-recruit when overlap exists

### MU Alumni Network
- **Channels:**
  - LinkedIn search for "University of Missouri" + "K–12" + "earth science" / "middle school science"
  - Mizzou Alumni Association communications
  - Specific cohorts of College of Education alumni in priority states
- **How to engage:**
  - First step: ask the College of Education whether they can include MHS in alumni communications
  - Second step: targeted LinkedIn outreach to alumni in priority states

### Mizzou DoE-Funded Peer Projects
- **Why it matters:** other Missouri-led DoE grants in K–12 STEM may have teacher networks that overlap with our target audience.
- **Engagement:** identify peer projects via NSF and Department of Education award databases; explore shared recruitment channels

## Tier 3 — Curriculum Publishers

K–12 publishers selling earth-science middle-school curriculum have existing teacher networks and direct sales channels into districts.

### Major K–12 Science Publishers
- **Pearson** — large; multiple earth-science middle-school product lines
- **McGraw-Hill** — large; Glencoe earth-science legacy products
- **HMH (Houghton Mifflin Harcourt)** — large
- **Cengage / National Geographic Learning** — earth-science content with NatGeo cachet
- **Discovery Education** — strong middle-school science presence; absorbed Mystery Science (cross-reference [competitive-landscape.md](competitive-landscape.md))

### Engagement
- **Co-marketing pitches:** "MHS supplements your X earth-science curriculum with NGSS-aligned game-based learning"
- **Caveat:** publishers may see MHS as competitive rather than complementary depending on framing. Lead with "research project, free to participants" to defuse.
- **Partner status as reference customer:** publishers reaching middle-school earth-science teachers may have an interest in being seen alongside DoE-funded research.

### Smaller / Niche Publishers
- **Stile Education** — middle-school science with NGSS alignment
- **Carolina Biological** — long-standing science teaching materials; broad teacher list
- **Lab-Aids** — inquiry-based curriculum publisher, NGSS-aligned

## Tier 4 — Apple Distinguished Educators (ADE) and Schools

Cross-reference with [apple-education-contacts.md](apple-education-contacts.md). Apple's well-organized network of digital-learning teachers includes earth-science teachers.

### Apple Distinguished Educators
- **What it is:** Apple's selective educator program; directory is publicly visible
- **Recruiting fit:** strong — these teachers actively use iPads and are research-friendly by selection
- **How to engage:**
  - Identify ADEs teaching middle-school earth science via the directory
  - Direct outreach with personalized messages
- **Counterpart on Chromebook side:** Google for Education Innovators (see [chromebook-channels.md](chromebook-channels.md))

### Apple Distinguished Schools
- **What they are:** schools recognized by Apple for digital-learning leadership; many run 1:1 iPad programs
- **Recruiting fit:** strong — institutional buy-in is already established; teachers in these schools have administrative support for research participation

## Tier 5 — Federal Agencies and Federally Funded Networks

### NOAA Office of Education
- **What it is:** federal agency K–12 education programs; relevant because of MHS's earth-science / hydrology focus
- **Engagement:** explore Sea Grant teacher networks, B-WET (Bay Watershed Education and Training) program teachers
- **Recruiting fit:** strong for the watershed/water-cycle elements of MHS

### USGS Education
- **What it is:** USGS K–12 educator resources and programs
- **Engagement:** newsletter and educator network access
- **Recruiting fit:** earth-science alignment

### NASA Earth Science Education
- **What it is:** NASA's earth-science K–12 programs (My NASA Data, GLOBE, etc.)
- **Engagement:** GLOBE program in particular has a teacher network well-aligned with MHS
- **Recruiting fit:** moderate to strong; depends on GLOBE program engagement

### National Science Foundation DRK-12 Network
- **What it is:** NSF's Discovery Research K–12 program funds K–12 STEM education research; peer projects often share recruiting channels
- **Engagement:** identify peer DRK-12 grantees in earth-science middle-school space via NSF Award Search
- **Recruiting fit:** strong for partnership channels; less direct for teacher recruiting

### Department of Education Education Innovation and Research (EIR) Network
- **What it is:** the federal program likely funding MHS
- **Engagement:** the program managers may know peer projects with overlapping teacher targets
- **Recruiting fit:** indirect — partnership / network access more than direct recruiting

## Tier 6 — Other DoE / NSF–Funded Earth-Science Education Projects

Peer projects often co-recruit when their teacher targets overlap. Search for:

- DoE EIR or i3 / Investing in Innovation grantees in middle-school science
- NSF DRK-12 grantees with earth-science middle-school focus
- NSF AISL (Advancing Informal STEM Learning) earth-science projects

For each peer project found:
- Identify PI and project URL
- Check for teacher recruitment language on their site
- Reach out to PI about co-recruiting if scope is non-competitive

## Risk and Considerations

- **Mizzou political navigation.** Working through internal Mizzou channels requires understanding which offices to engage in what order. Outreach office, Dean's office, and PI should be aligned before mass communication goes out.
- **Publisher competitive framing.** Avoid framing MHS as a publisher's competitor; lead with "research project, free to teachers." Publishers have killed similar projects' partner pitches by treating them as competitive products.
- **NGSS organization gatekeeping.** BSCS / NextGenScience are influential; alignment with their frameworks adds credibility but also requires meeting their standards. EQuIP rubric review for MHS curriculum may be worth pursuing for credibility.
