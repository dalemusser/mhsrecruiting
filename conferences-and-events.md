# Conferences and Events

Conferences and events where middle-school earth-science teachers gather. Conferences serve three recruiting functions: in-person teacher reach, presenter platform for credibility, and partnership / network building with other earth-science orgs.

> **Status note:** This file is a scaffold. **All dates, locations, and deadlines need to be verified** by checking each conference's current website. Conference scheduling shifts year to year. See [info-gathering-plan.md](info-gathering-plan.md) Priority 2.

---

## How to Use a Conference for Recruiting

For each conference relevant to the campaign, decide between four engagement levels:

1. **Attend** — send one or two team members; meet teachers, exchange contacts.
2. **Exhibit** — booth at the exhibit hall. Costs more; reaches more people. Need printed flyers, demo laptop / iPad, sign-up sheet.
3. **Present** — submit a session proposal. Highest credibility lift. Requires meeting the conference's CFP deadline (often 6+ months ahead).
4. **Sponsor** — paid sponsorship of conference materials, sessions, or events. Highest visibility, highest cost.

For Fall 2026 cohort, conference participation is mostly behind us (proposal deadlines for fall NSTAs were typically spring/early summer 2026). Conferences become the primary channel for **Spring 2027 recruiting**.

## Tier 1 — National Science Education Conferences

### NSTA National Conference
- **What it is:** the largest U.S. science teacher conference
- **Frequency:** annual, typically late March / early April
- **Audience:** ~10,000+ K–12 science teachers, many earth-science specifically
- **2026:** **VERIFY** date and location (likely past for 2026)
- **2027:** **VERIFY** date — submit proposal as soon as CFP opens (typically 9 months before)
- **Engagement options:**
  - Attend: $$ (registration + travel)
  - Exhibit: $$$
  - Present: free if accepted; CFP deadline is the gate
- **Recruiting fit:** strong — large audience, significant earth-science presence, NESTA-sponsored sessions
- **Recommendation:** target 2027 national for presentation; consider exhibit booth if budget allows

### NSTA Regional Conferences (Fall)
- **What they are:** three regional NSTA conferences each fall in different U.S. cities
- **2026 cities:** **VERIFY** — historical rotation includes Boston, Atlanta, Long Beach, Reno, Seattle, Cincinnati, Kansas City
- **Audience:** ~2,000–4,000 each, regional but with national speakers
- **CFP deadlines:** typically late spring / early summer for fall conferences
- **Recruiting fit:** strong for the region; manageable scale
- **Recommendation:** prioritize the region with the strongest MHS-relevant fit (e.g., near Mizzou for Missouri-region testimony; near California for largest market). Submit at least one proposal.

### NESTA-Sponsored Sessions at NSTA
- **What they are:** NESTA runs a "Friends of Earth Science" session and other earth-science-specific sessions at NSTA national and regional conferences
- **Engagement:** apply through NESTA rather than NSTA directly
- **Recruiting fit:** ideal — exact audience match
- **Recommendation:** highest priority conference channel

## Tier 2 — Educational Technology and Game-Based Learning

### ISTE
- **What it is:** the largest educational technology conference
- **Frequency:** annual, typically late June / early July
- **Audience:** ~15,000+ educators with tech focus; significant Chromebook and iPad presence
- **2026:** **VERIFY** date and location
- **CFP deadline:** typically October for the following summer
- **Recruiting fit:** strong for Chromebook-heavy districts and tech-forward teachers; somewhat less concentrated on earth science
- **Recommendation:** worth attending in 2027 if Spring cohort recruiting is needed; presentation needs early proposal

### Games + Learning + Society (GLS)
- **What it is:** academic conference at the intersection of games, learning, and society
- **Frequency:** annual; recently revived after COVID disruption
- **Audience:** smaller (hundreds), heavily research-leaning; some K–12 teacher attendance
- **Recruiting fit:** moderate — fits MHS's research framing well, but smaller direct teacher reach
- **Recommendation:** present a research methods session; doubles as academic credibility and networking with peer DoE-funded projects

### Games for Change (G4C)
- **What it is:** annual festival of games for social impact, including education
- **Frequency:** annual, typically June in NYC
- **Audience:** mixed game industry, education, and social-impact community
- **Recruiting fit:** moderate — good for partnership building, lower for direct teacher recruitment
- **Recommendation:** networking attendance; not a primary recruiting channel

Highest-priority state conferences (cross-reference with [state-channels.md](state-channels.md)):

## Tier 4 — Earth-Science-Specific Events

### Earth Science Week (October)
- **What it is:** annual outreach campaign coordinated by AGI; thousands of teachers participate with classroom activities
- **Frequency:** annual, mid-October
- **Engagement:** become a sponsoring organization, listed resource, or featured partner
- **Recruiting fit:** ideal for Spring 2027 cohort recruiting — reaches earth-science teachers exactly when they're most engaged
- **Recommendation:** pursue partner inclusion for 2026 (deadline likely summer); see [earth-science-organizations.md](earth-science-organizations.md) AGI entry

### NESTA Standalone Events
- **What they are:** NESTA workshops, virtual events, and the annual "Friends of Earth Science" gathering at NSTA
- **Engagement:** member or sponsored partner
- **Recruiting fit:** ideal — direct audience match
- **Recommendation:** see [earth-science-organizations.md](earth-science-organizations.md)

### Geological Society of America (GSA) Annual Meeting Education Sessions
- **What it is:** GSA's annual meeting includes K–12 education sessions
- **Frequency:** annual fall
- **Recruiting fit:** moderate — research-heavy audience; some K–12 teacher reach via NAGT-sponsored events at GSA
- **Recommendation:** lower priority unless presenting a research methods piece

## Budget and Tradeoffs

A rough budget estimate (assumes ~2026 prices, single attendee per event):

| Engagement type | Per event | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Attend (NSTA national / ISTE) | $1,500–2,500 | registration + flight + 3 nights |
| Attend (state conference) | $400–800 | typically drive-in for nearby states |
| Exhibit (NSTA national) | $5,000–15,000+ | booth + materials + travel |
| Present (any conference) | cost = attend + slides prep | free admission for accepted presenters in some cases |
| Sponsor (event sponsorship) | $1,000–25,000+ | depends on tier |

For the campaign:
- **Minimum viable:** present at 1 NSTA regional (fall 2026); attend 1–2 state conferences in priority states. ~$3,000–5,000 budget.
- **Recommended:** above plus exhibit at 1 fall NSTA regional; partner inclusion for Earth Science Week 2026. ~$10,000–15,000 budget.
- **Aggressive:** above plus NSTA national 2027 presentation + booth; attendance at CSTA, CAST, STANYS. ~$25,000+ budget.

Decisions on conference budget level should be made early — most CFP deadlines are inflexible.
