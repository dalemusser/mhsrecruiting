# Teacher Value Proposition

What teachers actually get for participating in Mission HydroSci. This file is the source of truth — outreach templates, the landing page, the one-pager, and webinar talking points should all match what's documented here.

The strongest pitch combines **monetary** (stipend), **professional** (PD credit, co-authorship), and **classroom** (free curriculum, training, support) value. Most teachers respond more to the professional and classroom items than to the stipend at the margin, so confirming items currently marked "for consideration" would meaningfully strengthen the campaign.

## For Consideration (Recommend Confirming)

These are not confirmed but would meaningfully strengthen the pitch. The MHS team should evaluate whether each can be offered, and if so, document terms here.

### PD Credit / Clock Hours
- **Why it matters:** PD credit is often the deciding factor for teachers in states with strict re-certification requirements. Many teachers will join for PD credit alone.
- **Implementation options:**
  - **University of Missouri PD certificate** — easiest path; the Mizzou College of Education can issue PD certificates for hours of training and deployment work. Most states honor university-issued PD documentation for some portion of re-certification.
  - **State-by-state PD partnerships** — slower but more definitive. Partner with state DoEs in priority states to formally count MHS as PD hours.
  - **Generic certificate of completion** — fallback. Acceptable in some states; less valuable than the above.
- **Recommended action:** offer a Mizzou-issued certificate by default, and pursue state partnerships in CA, TX, NY, FL, IL during Spring 2027 recruiting.
- **Outreach use:** "Receive a PD certificate from the University of Missouri College of Education for participation."

### Co-Authorship / Named Acknowledgment
- **What this means:** participating teachers receive named acknowledgment in research publications and conference presentations. Possibly co-authorship on practitioner-facing publications (NSTA journals, etc.).
- **Why it matters:** for early-career teachers, this is a significant CV item. For teachers seeking leadership roles, it differentiates them.
- **Implementation:** depends on the project's research publication plans. Even a simple "thanked in acknowledgments" line is valuable; co-authorship on practitioner pieces is more impactful.
- **Recommended action:** offer named acknowledgment as default; co-authorship on opt-in basis for teachers who want to contribute beyond classroom deployment.
- **Outreach use:** "Participating teachers are recognized in research publications resulting from this work."

### University of Missouri Letter of Participation
- **What this means:** a formal letter on Mizzou letterhead, signed by the PI, documenting the teacher's participation, role, and dates.
- **Why it matters:** useful for teacher portfolios, evaluations, license renewal applications, and tenure files. Low cost to provide; high perceived value.
- **Recommended action:** offer to all participating teachers automatically; mention in outreach.
- **Outreach use:** "Receive a formal letter of participation from the University of Missouri."

### Equipment Loan
- **What this means:** if a participating teacher's school is short on iPads or Chromebooks needed to run MHS, the project can loan equipment for the deployment period.
- **Why it matters:** removes the largest single barrier for under-resourced schools. Can convert "interested but can't" into "yes."
- **Implementation:** depends on whether MHS has a budget for equipment loan pool. Even a small pool (10–20 devices) used strategically would have outsized impact.
- **Recommended action:** confirm whether this is feasible. If yes, advertise selectively (don't promise broadly) — use as a closer for high-value schools.

### Travel Stipend for Training or Conference
- **What this means:** if any in-person training or conference participation is offered, MHS covers travel for participating teachers.
- **Why it matters:** in-person engagement deepens commitment and produces better testimonials and referrals downstream.
- **Implementation:** depends on training format and grant budget.
- **Recommended action:** evaluate whether even one in-person event (e.g., a one-day summer institute) is feasible. If yes, advertise prominently.

### Free Department Access After Cohort
- **What this means:** after the cohort, the teacher's full department gets free access to MHS for the following school year.
- **Why it matters:** multiplier effect — turns one teacher into a department evangelist. Solves the "I'd love this for my colleagues too" objection.
- **Implementation:** if MHS will be free or low-cost post-research anyway, this is essentially free; if there's a planned commercial model, this is a real concession.
- **Recommended action:** confirm pricing model. If free post-research, offer this universally. If not, offer to teachers from large schools selectively.

### Recognition on Project Website
- **What this means:** participating teachers listed (with consent) on the MHS website as cohort members.
- **Why it matters:** small but tangible recognition. Many teachers value this more than expected.
- **Recommended action:** offer to all; opt-in basis. Adds a "social proof" element to the landing page.

## How to Use This in Outreach

For each outreach channel and template, use the value proposition selectively:

- **Cold teacher outreach:** lead with "stipend, free curriculum and training," then mention PD credit and co-authorship as secondary
- **District / administrator outreach:** lead with "free curriculum, training, and ongoing support — no cost to your district"; mention research participation as secondary
- **Newsletter / mass channels:** lead with the headline "Stipend + free curriculum + PD credit," with details on the landing page
- **Pilot teacher referrals:** lead with the human story (their experience), then list value props in the forwardable email
- **Conference presentations:** integrate into the closing slide with QR code to apply

---

## What Not to Promise

To protect credibility, do not promise items that aren't confirmed yet. Each outreach message should match exactly what's in the "Confirmed Value" section — no more, no less — until items in "For Consideration" are confirmed.

Common pitfalls to avoid:
- Promising specific dollar amounts before they're set
- Promising PD credit will count in a specific state without confirming with that state
- Promising equipment loan as a default — promise selectively only if the loan pool exists
- Promising co-authorship as default — promise acknowledgment as default, co-authorship as opt-in
