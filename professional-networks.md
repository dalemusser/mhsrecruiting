# Professional Networks & Curriculum Programs

Professional development organizations, curriculum networks, and media programs that reach middle school science teachers. Useful both as direct outreach surfaces and as channels for finding teachers already engaged in active PD or inquiry-based instruction.

---

## Professional Development Organizations

### Teacher Institute for Evolutionary Science (TIES)
- **Site:** https://tieseducation.org
- **Provides:** Workshops for middle school science teachers, NGSS-aligned instruction
- **Why it matters for MHS:** Direct access to active middle school science teachers and their professional development networks. Teachers participating in formal PD are often more receptive to research participation because the workflow is already part of their professional practice.

### PRISM (Indiana Teacher Resource Portal)
- **Site:** https://www.rose-prism.org/
- **Built for:** Middle school science teachers
- **Approach:** Links state and national standards directly to classroom activities
- **Why it matters for MHS:** Strong signal of how teachers think about curriculum alignment and classroom expectations. Useful for understanding how to frame MHS standards alignment when reaching out to similar networks in other states.

---

## Media / Curriculum Programs Used by Teachers

### STEM in 30 (Smithsonian)
- **Site:** https://www.airandspace.si.edu/learn/stem-30
- **Audience:** Middle school
- **Format:** Short, classroom-friendly modules; used in classrooms nationwide
- **Why it matters for MHS:** Reflects the format teachers prefer for classroom-friendly delivery (short, engaging, standards-aligned). Useful as a partnership channel and as a reference for content format expectations.

---

## Patterns This Tells Us

- Teachers who actively participate in PD networks tend to be more receptive to research participation because they already engage with curriculum-development workflows.
- Standards alignment is the universal common language across these networks. NGSS in particular is the entry point for any middle school science conversation.
- Short, classroom-friendly, immediately usable content formats are the norm. MHS recruiting messages should emphasize how a teacher could deploy MHS without major curriculum disruption.

## Gaps to Fill in Future Work

The current list is small. Useful additions for the next round of recruiting research:

- **State-level science teacher associations** in target states (e.g., California Science Teachers Association, Texas Science Teachers Association, New York State Science Teachers Association). NSTA chapters by state are a natural starting point.
- **NSTA (National Science Teaching Association)** national programs and conferences.
- **NESTA (National Earth Science Teachers Association)** — directly aligned with MHS's earth-science focus.
- **NGSS-focused PD networks** (Achieve, NextGenScience, BSCS).
- **Regional / university-affiliated PD programs**, particularly any tied to the University of Missouri's College of Education that could offer warm introductions.
- **Teacher conference tracks** (NSTA national / regional conferences, ISTE) where MHS could be presented or demoed.
