# MHS Recruiting

Sources, contacts, and the campaign plan for recruiting middle school earth-science teachers to the **Mission HydroSci (MHS)** research project.

## About Mission HydroSci

Mission HydroSci is a Department of Education–funded research project led out of the Colleges of Education and Engineering at the **University of Missouri** (PI: Jim Laffey). It is an interactive, game-based learning experience designed to help middle school students build scientific reasoning and problem-solving skills through inquiry-driven gameplay focused on Earth Science.

The core research compares student learning in Earth Science topics between gameplay and traditional instruction, and explores how AI can be used to generate more meaningful feedback from student decisions — going beyond correctness to better understand how students are thinking.

**Project links:**
- Mission HydroSci — https://mhs.missouri.edu
- Intelligence Builders — https://intelligencebuilders.com
- Dale Musser — https://dalemusser.com

## Who We're Recruiting

- **Role:** Middle school earth-science teachers
- **Geography:** Any state in the USA
- **Timing:** Pilot deployments are running in Missouri now; broader classroom deployments are planned for **Fall 2026** (50+ teacher cohort target) and **Spring 2027**
- **Tech requirement:** Classrooms where students have access to **iPads or Chromebooks** (Mission HydroSci runs on either platform)

When recording prospective schools or districts, capture which device platform their students use — iPads, Chromebooks, or both. Any school with adequate technology to run Mission HydroSci is in scope.

## How to Use This Repo During the Campaign

### Week 1 — Foundation
1. Read **[recruiting-strategy.md](recruiting-strategy.md)** for the campaign plan.
2. Confirm **[value-proposition.md](value-proposition.md)** items with the MHS team (especially stipend amount, PD credit, co-authorship).
3. Use **[recruiting-infrastructure.md](recruiting-infrastructure.md)** as the checklist for what to build before Wave 1 outreach.
4. Activate **[pilot-teacher-engagement.md](pilot-teacher-engagement.md)** — this is the highest-ROI move.
5. Stand up **[tracking-template.md](tracking-template.md)** in Airtable.

### Weeks 2–3 — Research and Channel Activation
6. Work through **[info-gathering-plan.md](info-gathering-plan.md)** in priority order.
7. Use the channel-inventory files as scaffolds — fill in **VERIFY** and **TO RESEARCH** placeholders as you go.
8. Begin Wave 1 outreach using **[outreach-templates.md](outreach-templates.md)** and the warm channels in `pilot-teacher-engagement.md`, `teacher-resource-sites.md`, and `earth-science-organizations.md`.

### Weeks 4+ — Broad Campaign
9. Run Waves 2 and 3 per the timeline in `recruiting-strategy.md`.
10. Review channel ROI weekly; reallocate effort.
11. Use **[competitive-landscape.md](competitive-landscape.md)** to frame MHS for teachers already using other tools.
12. Lock the cohort by 2026-08-15.

### After Fall 2026 Lock
13. Repeat the cycle (compressed) for Spring 2027 recruiting, August through November 2026.
14. Use Fall 2026 cohort as testimonials and active referrers for Spring 2027.

## Working Principles

> The real center of gravity is not districts — it is teachers.

- **Teachers choose tools.** Direct teacher outreach beats district-level outreach for recruitment, even when district-level approval is also needed.
- **Pilot teachers are the cheapest channel.** Every other channel costs time or money; pilot referrals are nearly free and convert at higher rates.
- **iPads OR Chromebooks — both qualify.** Be inclusive in every outreach message; the Chromebook side is currently underrepresented in the channel inventory.
- **Track every contact.** A teacher who declines for Fall 2026 is a viable Spring 2027 candidate.
- **Don't promise what isn't confirmed.** See [value-proposition.md](value-proposition.md) — match outreach to the confirmed list, not the wishlist.
