# San Francisco Unified School District (SFUSD)

A large urban public school district in California, serving the City and County of San Francisco. SFUSD is a **1:1 Chromebook** district at the K–12 level, which makes every middle school in the district technically compatible with Mission HydroSci.

## Device Platform: Chromebook 1:1

SFUSD has implemented a district-wide 1:1 Chromebook initiative. Key details:

- **1:1 initiative.** As of 2024–2025, SFUSD aimed to be a 1:1 district — every student has an individual Chromebook.
- **Take-home program (Grades 4–12).** Students in grades 4 through 12 are typically assigned a personal Chromebook to bring to school and use for homework. **This includes all middle school grades (6–8), which is the MHS target.**
- **In-classroom use (Grades K–3).** Younger grades use stored, in-classroom devices rather than take-home assignments.
- **Summer retention.** Students often keep assigned Chromebooks over the summer to ensure readiness for the next school year, particularly 5th and 8th graders transitioning to new schools.
- **Restricted login.** SFUSD uses specialized login procedures, restricting devices to official student accounts.
- **Device support.** Families can request devices through SFUSD's dedicated technology-access support.

### Implication for MHS

- Every SFUSD middle school student has a Chromebook available for classroom and homework use.
- MHS Chromebook compatibility is a hard requirement for SFUSD — the iPad version is irrelevant here.
- The take-home assignment model is favorable: MHS gameplay can extend beyond the classroom period if a teacher chooses.
- Restricted-login policies mean any web-app deployment of MHS needs to work within SFUSD's account structure. Confirm with district IT before deployment.

## Research Approval Process

Like most large urban districts, SFUSD likely requires central approval for outside research before teachers / classrooms can participate, even when a specific school's leadership is willing.

- **What to research:** SFUSD's Research, Planning, and Assessment (or equivalent) office; their formal research application process; lead time
- **Why it matters:** if MHS recruits SFUSD teachers without district approval, the deployment could be blocked at the school level just before the cohort starts. Better to confirm the path early.
- **TO RESEARCH:**
  - SFUSD research review office contact (likely accessible via sfusd.edu)
  - Research application form / submission process
  - Typical approval lead time
  - Whether MHS's existing IRB approval (University of Missouri) is sufficient or whether SFUSD requires additional review

## Why SFUSD Is a High-Priority Target

1. **Chromebook 1:1 means scale.** Every middle school in the district has the technical infrastructure to run MHS without hardware barriers.
2. **California priority state.** Per [state-channels.md](state-channels.md).
3. **Geographic proximity to project lead.** Enables in-person engagement other priority districts don't allow.
4. **Visibility.** A successful SFUSD deployment becomes a high-profile reference customer for the broader campaign and for Spring 2027 recruiting.
5. **Diversity.** SFUSD's student body is highly diverse (linguistically, culturally, socioeconomically) — meaningful for the research's external validity.
